Jim Steed's Favorite Records of 2003

This list is no particular order. After spending the past couple years in a promo-induced coma, 2003 gave me a chance to take a breath and just see what's out there, and I found a few great mainstream records, a few great indie rock records, and a ton of great underground records.

  1. Bonnie "Prince" Billy Master and Everyone (Drag City)
    One of Oldham's most intimate records, almost as bare as his debut. Filled with the type of self loathing and self mocking that I can really identify with.
  2. Sunburned Hand of the Man Magnetic Drugs (Manhand)
    One of their better CDRs (but not as good as Wild Animal or Jaybird), this has a bit more of a jazz feel.
  3. Sunburned Hand of the Man Closer to the Bone (Manhand)
    One of their better CDRs (but not as good as Wild Animal or Jaybird), this has a bit more of a drone/drum circle feel.
  4. Outkast Speakerboxx/The Love Below (Arista)
    This just won Grammy for best album. What the fuck? I haven't listened to a hip hop album this much since the early 90s which either means "this is great" or "I have been ignoring hip hop for too long" (or both!).
  5. Sea and Cake, The One Bedroom (Thrill Jockey)
    An improvement over the bland Oui that feels like the little brother to classic The Fawn.
  6. Cat Power You Are Free (Matador)
    She is a prick, and it's okay to hate her, but how can you resist the "My Name is Luka" redux of "Names"?
  7. Lightning Bolt Wonderful Rainbow (Load)
    The albums are moving closer to the live show, which is great.
  8. Kinski Airs Above Your Station (Subpop)
    More thoughtful than their glorious Pacifico album Be Gentle With the Warm Turtle, showing more Kraut and more texture, but also a bit less pop around the edges.
  9. Landing Passages Through (K)
    Builds well off of Seasons.
  10. Surface of Eceyon Dragyyn (Strange Attractors)
    More rock than their debut. Why did everyone go ape shit over AMT but never listen to SOE?
  11. Thermals, The More Parts Per Million (Subpop)
    Lo-fi redux, but more energetic and punk.
  12. Yo La Tengo Summer Sun (Matador)
    Okay, so it's not as good as their landmark albums, and okay, it's not as good as their recent albums, but, hey, it helped pass more than a couple drab workdays.
  13. Mars Volta, The De-Loused in the Comatorium (Universal)
    Totally immense Led Zep/Pink Floyd inspired concept album. Admirably overzealous.
  14. Double Leopards Halve Maen (Eclipse)
    This double LP isn't as enveloping as A Pebble in Thousands of Unmapped Revolutions but still great.
  15. Wind-Up Bird, The WHIPS (Music Fellowship)
    A heartfelt drone record.
  16. Rapture, The Echoes (Universal)
    DFA turns The Rapture's twitch-punk version of early The Cure into an even discoer, dancier version of sell-out-era The Cure. And it's good.
  17. Four Tet Rounds (Domino)
    Pure, minimal, low-key electronica.
  18. Stevens, Sufjan Michigan (Asthmatic Kitty)
    Late year indie pop surprise that tries to do for Michigan what Brian Wilson did for California (good luck).
  19. Unicorns, The Who Will Cut Your Hair When We're Gone? (Alien8)
    Wow, it's 1998 again, and Elephant 6 is finally dying down, with its last gasp being more experimental bands like Music Tapes and Bablicon. No wait, it's 2003 and these Canucks are totally copping that vibe.
  20. Hala Strana s/t (Emperor Jones)
    Amazing folk/Eastern European record by Steven R. Smith. The Jewelled Antler CDRs are also great.

If you include albums that came out before 2003 but I didn't hear til 2003, the following items might have deserved a spot:

  • Tape - Opera (Hapna)
    Amazing electro-acoustic album such as Gastr del Sol and Fennesz
  • Sunburned Hand of the Man - Headdress (Records)
    A little abbreviated in the LP format
  • Jennifer Gentle - Funny Creatures Lane (Sillyboy)
    Great, crazy, weird Zappa-inspired indie rock and drone
  • Limp - Orion (Morr)
    Man from Manual's band is a lot like Manual
