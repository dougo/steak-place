dan ruccia's Favorite Records of 2003

  1. A Frames 2 (S-S)
  2. US Maple Purple on Time (Drag City)
    a "normal" record by us maple standards but still way out there
  3. Henry Flynt Back Porch Hillbilly Blues vol. 1 and 2 (Locust)
    I am a total sucked for Flynt's minimalist avant-country fiddling
  4. John Fahey Red Cross (Revenant)
    his version of summertime destroys me every time I hear it. as hauntingly gorgeous as anything he ever recorded
  5. Matmos Civil War (Matador)
  6. Aesop Rock Bazooka Tooth (Definitive Jux)
  7. The User Symphony no.2 for Dot Matrix Printers (Asphodel)
    makes me miss the old 386 we had when I was a kid... also of note is their album of music for the silophone (a massive abandoned grain silo near montreal that is equipped with speakers and microphones, which you can play at www.silophone.net), "Abandon"
  8. Eleventh Dream Day Prairie School Freakout (Thrill Jockey)
    I was about 5 when this record originally came out, so this was essentially a new find for me.
  9. Various Flowers in the Wildwood (Trikont)
    female country music from the 1920s-40s that is totally amazing
  10. William Parker Violin Trio Scrapbook (Thirsty Ear)
    easily the strongest album of the year on thirsty ear, a label that has become mired in the world hiphop/jazz/electronica fusion. parker, billy bang, and hamid drake make some outstanding free jazz without any wind intruments present
  11. The Hospitals s/t (In the Red)
    one of the best slabs of garage noise I've heard in a while
  12. Liaisons Dangereuses s/t (Hit Thing)
    awesome german new wave from 1981 with one of the guys in daf
  13. Lightning Bolt Wonderful Rainbow (Load)
    wonderful melodic noise
  14. Fe-mail Syklubb fra Haelvete (TV5)
    easily the best pure noise record of the year from a pair of norwegian women
  15. Stark Reality Now (Stones Throw)
    Hoagy Carmichael songs get reworked in 1970 in a strange funk/jazz/prog style that is made even stranger by some vibraphone work from outer space
  16. Starvations Get Well Soon (Gold Standard Labs)
    yes, they sound like the gun club, but they actually go somewhere with that style. sure, there are a few clunker tracks, but as a whole this is solid
  17. Epileptics/Flux of Pink Indians Fits and Starts (Dr Strange)
    two overlapping late 70s/early 80s punk/post-punk groups on one convenient cd
  18. Dizzee Rascal Boy in da Corner (Too Pure)
    this didn't get US release until 2004, but it officially came out in '03. this 19 year old makes some of the strangest hiphop I've ever heard, with amazing scuzzy beats and simple, yet effective, lyrics. I'm still puzzled by all the hype around this record, though
  19. Various Miami Sound (Soul Jazz)
    funk!
  20. Michael Yonkers Band Microminiature Love (Sub Pop)
