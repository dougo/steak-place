Every year I've been following independent music (basically a decade now),
there's always been a coterie of my pals who insist that it ain't like it
used to be, that it was so much better in the heyday of <insert any band or
label or city's musical flowering>.

Every other year, I've been able to pull about 4 or 5 examples out of the air
and say, no, you're just not paying attention, but '95 truly strikes me as an
off year, probably the first year since I've been following music in any form
(we're talking about since I was ten, here), that I can't come up w/10 albums
of the year I really loved. 

Or, I dunno, maybe I've become as jaded and cynical as my pals seemed to be.
I do know that for the first time in my life I got pretty sick of music and
the fucking circus that surrounds it (a feeling which, fortunately, has
proven to be temporary). I know that the rock ain't dead, not by a longshot,
and I'm not pining for '85, or '81, or '88. But things do seem a bit thin
these days, and my patience for the passable and
technically-good-but-unexciting is virtually nonexistent. The latter is
probably a good thing; the former kinda worries me.
