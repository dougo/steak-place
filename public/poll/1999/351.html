OLD

  • Anima~Sound "Musik für Alle" [Alga Marghen] Tractor-jazz?
  • Boards of Canada "Hi Scores" [Skam] An artifact worthy of its ebay stock. Chug-hot IDM too!
  • Captain Beefheart & his Magic Band "Grow Fins" [Revenant] Not exactly what it was proposed to have been, but the 'field recording' aspect does not muddy up the diamond/ carrot for this rabid rabbit.
  • Gal Costa "Cinema Olympia" [Philips] in which an international pop diva makes a lush swinging/ screaming psyche record that at one point turns into the last Boredoms record, all from that majickal year of '69.
  • Walt Dickerson Quartet "Impressions of 'A Patch of Blue'" [Verve] No celeste from sideman Ra, but the harpsichord-vibraphone interaction is gorgeous.
  • Marvin Gaye "Midnight Love & Sexual Healing Sessions" [Columbia] To fend off the vultures.
  • Giorgio Moroder "From Here to Eternity" [Castle Communications] Crucial link in Kraftwerk/ disco to New Order/ Pet Shop Boys chain.
  • Taj Mahal Travellers "August 1974" [P-Vine] Instant kif.
  • Masayuki "Jojo" Takayanagi & New Direction for the Arts "Free Form Suite" [three blind mice] Over the Abe duet and _The Call in Question_, for its progression from subtle blues beauty to all-out amoebic feedback and dual drum splatter.
  • Los Zafiros "Bossa Cubana" [Nonesuch] Doo-wop from another planet. No wait, Cuba. Ignacio sings from heaven.

NEW

  • Mike Anderson "Zalvi" LP [New World of Sound] One-sided, one-eyed monster, with tears flowing for Ayler. A mind-blowing meld of jazz outness and four-track fuckery, a rarely taken path.
  • Aurora Plastics Co. "Low Noise" [Bobby J] Near lost 70's private ish aesthetic that is a plastic mess.
  • Autechre "ep7" [Warp] Crunchy.
  • fenessz "+47°6'37" -165°'08"" [Touch] Vertigo to go.
  • köhn "2" [K-RAA-K] I'm a sucker for cracked electronics and dizzy dittiness.
  • Knife in the Water "plays one sound and others" [self-released] Local outfit that continues to suck the breath from me. Cohen slashes his wrists with Parsons/ Harris' teeth.
  • Ricky Martin "Cup of Life" [?] At least until I found out that it was about the World Cup, and not an arena-sized earth ritual chant.
  • Matmos "The West" [Deluxe] Banjos, bibles, AND breakbeats?
  • Mos Def "Black on Both Sides" [Rawkus] What great rap record is without an overbite?
  • No Neck Blues Band "Live at Ken's Electric Lake" [S@1] Oh, so that's how they eat a peach.
