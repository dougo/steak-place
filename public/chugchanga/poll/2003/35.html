Doug Orleans's Favorite Records of 2003

Usually I sort my list from best to worst, but I just couldn't bring myself to make those fine distinctions this year. So the list below is simply separated into top 20, top 50, and the rest, alphabetized within each category. All of the top 50 records are highly recommended, and most of the rest are really good too; there are a few "situational" records, or ones I mainly like just for a few tracks, but they're all worth checking out. Those who thought this was a weak year are totally wrong; maybe there weren't many true masterpieces, but the sheer volume of quality releases is impressive.

Now here's the obligatory list of records that either just didn't grab me or I actively disliked:

  • Andrew W.K. The Wolf
    I just don't get the appeal. Too incredibly cheesy for me to even appreciate ironically.
  • Natacha Atlas Something Dangerous
    Some good techno-ish moments here ("Daymalhum", "Like the Last Drop"), but the rest is syrupy overproduced "world music".
  • The Coral The Coral
    Some sort of sea-shanty/pirate rock, which sounds like a neat idea but it doesn't really work in practice.
  • The Dead C The Damned
    Try as I might, I just can't get into the Dead C. A couple tracks from this are OK ("Truth" and "Heat") but the rest just exceeds my tolerance for atonal arrhythmic feedbacky cacophany. I still love Skullflower, though.
  • Delays Hey Girl
    I like their Nearer Than Heaven EP, but this one has just a bit too much of the '80s college rock sound for me to stomach.
  • Missy Elliott This Is Not A Test!
    I liked "Get Ur Freak On" and a few other singles, but these tracks are all either dull or annoying.
  • Thee Midniters Greatest
    I thought they were a surf/garage band for some reason, but this is mostly watered-down soul. "Land of a Thousand Dances" is the highlight, but it's still a pretty weak version.
  • Spiritualized Amazing Grace
    Not as terrible as Let It Come Down, but still pretty lame. It's becoming clear that Ladies and Gentlemen We Are Floating In Space was not a breath of new life but more like a last gasp.
  • The Strokes Room On Fire
    Less annoying than Is This It?, but I'll chalk that up as a sophomore slump.
