Joseph J Willette's Favorite Records of 2003

Lots of reissues and odd stuff in my list this year...

[21-30 are honorable mentions --dougo]

  1. Dj Z-Trip Live in Los Angeles (Self Released)
    Who cares if he's mixing other people's stuff, this guy has taken it to a whole new level and has me liking stuff I wouldn't like unmixed. Brilliant mix of Outkast and Tool for starters.
  2. Jesus & Mary Chain BBC: Live in Concert (Strange Fruit)
    Everybody seems to have missed this fanmtastic recording of JMC at their peak.
  3. Pluramon Dreams Top Rock (Karaoke)
    I think this is the best of the shoegaze/IDM releases, beating M83 for me.
  4. Cedric Im Brooks Light of Saba (Honest Jon)
    My fave of this year's reggae reissues
  5. Lightning Bolt Wonderful Rainbow (Load)
    The DVD was great - now we can enoy the tunes. Sounds much more live and rock n roll oriented than their previous releases to my ears
  6. Brian Jonestown Massacre And This is Our Music (TeePee)
    Great tracks as usual. Their tomfoolery will get wider exposure with the success of Dig! at this year's sundance.
  7. Mickey and the Soul Generation Iron Leg (Quannum)
    Great Funk oldies!
  8. Various Artists Nice Up the Dance (Soul Jazz)
    Fantastic Jagged Dancehall comp
  9. Blacktop I Got a Baaad Feelin... (In the Red)
    Mick Collins reissue. I liked it bettter than the new Dirtbombs disc.
  10. DJ Shadow Live in Austin (Mothers Milk)
    Why are his unofficial releases so much better than his official ones?
  11. Why? Live (Anticon)
    Much better than either of his 2 official releases. Sounds sort of like a twisted rock band here. Available only through the Anticon website (why???).
  12. Bug Pressure (Tigerbeat)
    Breakthrough in manic Dancehall/IDM
  13. Alias Muted (Anticon)
    Great indtrumentals. Also try the instrumental version of "other side of the looking glass: if you can find it.
  14. Guided By Voices Hardcore UFO's Box (Matador)
    Some great unreleased gems.
  15. Teledubgnosis Magnetic Learning (Wordsound)
    Great live sounding dub. May be Wordsound's best release! Criminally slept on.
  16. Roosevelt Tharpa ELaborate Neurotics (Goodnight)
    Fantastic example of Canadien Hip Hop
  17. Viktor Vaugn (MF Doom) Vaudeville Villians (Traffic)
    MF Doom is finally reaching his potential
  18. Pelican Pelican (Hydra Head)
    The EP worked better than the full length for me
  19. Sound Murderer and SK-1 Rewind (Rewind)
    Great use of Public Enemy in a Dancehall mix.
  20. Kid Koala Some of My Best Friends are DJ's (Ninja Tune)
    Intricate turntable stuff
  21. Wrens Meadowlands
    yes I do like pleasant guitar music too!
  22. Bomaar Monk DVD+ CD
    Anticon site only release with an old school mix of instrumentals with wacky videos
  23. Dirtbombs Dangerous Magical Noise
  24. M83 Dead Cities
  25. Various Artists Miami Sound (Soul Jazz)
    another great Soul Jazz comp
  26. Neil Michael Hagerty Howling Hex
    finally!
  27. Prefuse 73 both Extinguisher releases
  28. Little Killers (Crypt)
    Nice debut on Crypt
  29. DM & Gemini Non Prophets (Lex)
    2 of several great hip hop releases on the new Lex label-great packaging too
  30. Sole-Selling Live Water Themselves remixed
    more good stuff from Anticon
