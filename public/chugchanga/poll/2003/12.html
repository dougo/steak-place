Kathy Fennessy's Favorite Records of 2003

30.10.76.

I go to see my first proper punk group. I know what it's going to be like: I've been waiting for years, and this year most of all: something to match the explosions in my head. The group are called the Clash; everybody I talk to says they're the best.

Into a Victorian Hall, half empty with people standing in bunches. Hostile, insecure. Suddently four men with brutally cut hair come on stage, bark into a microphone, start making an industrial noise. The noise coalesces with the speed into a perfect chaos. One song: a genuine cry, a child screaming in fear: "Waa waa wanna waa waa." Within ten seconds I'm transfixed, within thirty, changed forever.

-- Jon Savage on "White Riot," England's Dreaming

[21 and below are Honorable Mentions. --dougo]

  1. The Dirtbombs Dangerous Magical Noise (In The Red)
    Mick Collins and gang give up the garage for glam to great effect.
  2. The White Stripes Elephant (V2)
    Detroit rules (see above).
  3. Holly Golightly Truly She Is None Other (Sympathy for the Record Industry)
    The underrated Brit’s 11th--and best--release.
  4. The Shins Chutes Too Narrow (Sub Pop)
    Portland transplants trade in the Zombies for the Byrds & the Hollies.
  5. Benjamin Biolay Negatif (Virgin France)
    Serge Gainsbourg's heir apparent makes good on his promise with this two-CD set.
  6. Stephen Malkmus & the Jicks Pig Lib (Matador)
    Malkmus & gang go prog. And I didn’t think I liked prog-rock...
  7. Broadcast Ha Ha Sound (Warp)
    Slinky and seductive. As odd as it is pretty.
  8. Quasi Hot Shit (Touch & Go)
    A rollicking collision between punk, classic rock, prog--& protest.
  9. Various Artists City of God [Original Soundtrack] (Milan)
    Brazilians go funk, glam--& prog. Great movie, too.
  10. The Clientele The Violet Hour (Merge)
    More slinky seduction from this throwback to Creation Records' heyday.
  11. Nicolai Dunger Tranquil Isolation (Overcoat Recordings)
    My favorite Swedish former pro soccer-playin' folkie.
  12. Aislers Set How I Learned to Write Backwards (Suicide Squeeze)
    My only complaint: it's too short.
  13. Blacktop I've Got a Baaad Feeling About This [Reissue] (In The Red)
    More garage-oriented Mick Collins goodness.
  14. Skip Bifferty The Story of Skip Bifferty [Reissue] (Castle)
    A must for fans of the Pretty Things & the Creation.
  15. Broadcast Pendulum [EP] (Warp)
    The louder, more experimental side of Broadcast.
  16. Rock City Rock City [Reissue] (Lucky Seven/Rounder)
    The rockin' seeds of the late, great Big Star.
  17. Swell Whenever You're Ready (Beggar's Banquet)
    After all these years--they're still...swell.
  18. Various Artists The Man Without a Past [Original Soundtrack] (Milan)
    Another great Aki Kaurismaki soundtrack--with *two* tracks by the Renegades!
  19. Brokeback Looks at the Bird (Thrill Jockey)
    Dreamy little release, featuring vocals from Laetitia Sadier & the late Mary Hansen.
  20. Various Artists Morvern Callar [Original Soundtrack] (Warp)
    This fine soundtrack includes *four* Holger Czukay/Can tracks (Alan Warner's book is dedicated to the man).
  21. Calexico Feast of Wire (Quarterstick/Touch & Go)
  22. Ben Charest Belleville Rendez-vous [Original Soundtrack to The Triplets of Belleville (Mute)
  23. Loose Fur S/T (Thrill Jockey)
  24. Four Tet Rounds (Domino)
  25. Al Green I Can't Stop (Blue Note)
  26. Various Artists Bay Area Funk (Luv 'n' Haight)
  27. Biff Bang Pow Waterbomb [Best Of] (Rev-Ola)
  28. Owl & the Pussycat S/T (K)
  29. Various Artists Lost in Translation [Original Soundtrack] (Emperor Norton)
  30. Nina Nastasia Run to Ruin (Touch & Go)
  31. Various Artists Kill Bill, Vol. I [Original Soundtrack] (A Band Apart/Maverick/WMG Soundtracks)
  32. Essex Green The Long Goodbye (Merge)
  33. Various Artists Pop Pop Pigalle (Pop Pop Pigalle)
  34. Visqueen King Me (BlueDisguise)
